The Pioneer of the UFC: Connor Mcgregor

UFC Debut fight: Marcus Brimage

This was McGregor debut to UFC and needing to make an splash into the scene if he was to continue growing his audience from Cage warriors. Although his debut McGregor looked incredibly comfortable, throwing loose and fast shots while weaving in and out of oncoming blows, Brimage stood no chance. After a swift first round knockout this was the start of McGregors knockout reputation in UFC.

Connor McGregor vs Max Holloway

As the only fight to break the pattern, this fight went for three rounds after max survived the first standing round he took it to the ground and tried to outwork McGregor, using varying guards to try find a weakness in McGregors game, however after McGregor passes and gains mount its all over for Max. A point of proof that Mcgregors ground game wasnt as bad as all the talk at the time suggested. He didnt earn his brown belt for nothing.

Connor McGregor vs Diego Brandeo

The fight began explosivly exchanging blows but after Diego shoots for a double leg the fight took a slower turn until McGregor gained top position on the ground where Diego took signifcant shots and another first round knockout shortly followed.

Connor McGregor vs Dustin Porier 1

This fight was just like the rest, with McGregor calling Poriers fighting and movement average and touting his ability to out manuver and find shots from anywhere, this was then backed up by a swift and confident display from McGregor.

Connor McGregor vs Dennis Silver

Similar the previous, McGregor picked his oppoent apart shot by shot, leaving him cut bruised and bleeding, Silver fell like the rest. Mcgregor finishes the fight by hopping the cage and staring down with Jose Aldo, the featherweight champion before demanding a title shot from the UFC owner himself Dana white

Interim Championship shot: Connor McGregor vs Chad Mendes

In the lead up to Aldo Vs McGregor for the title, Aldo suffered a rib injury and needed to pull out the fight. This left chad Mendes the opportunity to earn a title shot against Aldo and Connor to prove he was infact worthy of the title shot.
This fight had alot of disscussion with Mendes being the first strong and renowned wrestler he woudl face, his defensive grappling skills were put to the test before he later landed a knockout like the rest of his opponents.

Title Fight: Connor McGregor vs Jose Aldo

The first round began. McGregor sidekicks the leg. Aldo comes in eating a counter straight left dropping unconscious, McGregor lands a right hammerfist and it's over. 10 years of Jose Aldos reign over in 13 seconds. The first of Mcgregors titles, the first record he would break, just as McGregor said he would from a young age he became the featherweight champion.

Who is Conor Mcgregor?

Born: 14 July 1988
Wins: 22
Total: 28
Height: 5 ft 9 in (175 cm)
Debut date: 6 April 2013
Weight: 71 kg
Reach: 74 in



After growing up in Dublin ireland and learning martial arts to defend himself from bullies and street fights, Connor became obsessed with martial arts and proceeded to dominate the competition, consistently naming exactly which rounds he would knock his opponenets out and then living up to it.
His success garnered thousands of fans to watch him perform and listen to his press conferences where he would humiliate and belittle his upcoming oppoent, exploding the sport to the mainstream media, cermenting UFC into western culture for good.

In his time he set the worlds fastest knock out of thirteen seconds aswell as the worlds first UFC fighter to maintain two belts at the same time.
